## SECTORS & MATCHED_SECTORS

### Core principle
Assign a sector when full profile context makes it clear the person works with clients in that industry — the sector name need not appear explicitly. Use client names, project descriptions, industry terminology, role titles, practice group names, and website fields.

Examples: "NHS contract negotiations" → Healthcare; "advised on J.P. Morgan's acquisition of First Republic" → Financial Services; "EBITDA expansion at a national grocery chain" → Consumer & Retail.

### Adjacency mistakes to avoid
- **Data centers / digital infrastructure** → Technology, NOT Real Estate (only assign Real Estate when profile explicitly references property transactions, development, asset management, or property-sector clients)
- **Food & Beverage** → NOT Consumer & Retail (requires explicit retail stores, apparel, electronics, or non-food consumer categories)
- **Pharmaceuticals** → NOT Healthcare (requires explicit hospitals, clinics, providers, or payers)
- **Automotive** → NOT Industrials & Manufacturing (requires explicit manufacturing or heavy-industry evidence beyond automotive)

### Disambiguation when multiple matched_sectors strings fit the same evidence
Prefer in order:
1. **Most specific match** (e.g., "Pharmaceutical" over "Life Sciences" when work is clearly drug-focused; "Life Sciences" over "Pharmaceutical" when work spans biotech, diagnostics, and devices)
2. **Most frequently evidenced** in the profile
3. **Alphabetically first** as deterministic fallback

Include multiple strings only when distinct evidence supports each.

### `sectors` (free text)
Each entry is a **distinct top-level industry** — not a sub-specialization or topic.

**Consolidation rule:** merge multiple types of work within the same industry into a single entry.
- ❌ Wrong: `["education policy", "education technology", "k-12 education"]` (one industry)
- ✅ Correct: `["education"]`
- ✅ Correct: `["healthcare", "financial services"]` (genuinely distinct industries)

Use short plain industry names (1–3 words). If a `sectors` entry has no match in `<matched_sectors>`, include the free-text entry only.

### `matched_sectors` (controlled vocabulary)
Use exact strings from the `<matched_sectors>` list. Do NOT modify them. Apply the disambiguation hierarchy when multiple strings fit the same evidence.

**CRITICAL — vocabulary mixing:** `<sectors>` and `<matched_sectors>` are TWO SEPARATE lists with different strings. A value that appears in `<sectors>` (e.g. "Technology & Software", "Financial Services") is NEVER valid in `matched_sectors`. A value from the profile text (e.g. "Enterprise Software", "SaaS", "Cloud Computing") is NEVER valid in `matched_sectors` unless it appears verbatim in `<matched_sectors>`.

**Technology/software mapping guide** (common source of errors):
- Evidence of software, SaaS, enterprise tech, IT, cloud, or digital platforms → `Computing, Technology, Robotics & AI` (exact string in `<matched_sectors>`)
- Evidence of hardware, semiconductors, electronics manufacturing → `Electronics & Electrical`
- Evidence of telecom networks, carriers, ISPs → `Telecommunications`
- "Enterprise Software", "Technology & Software", "Technology", "Computing", "SaaS", "Cloud" are NOT valid `<matched_sectors>` strings — use `Computing, Technology, Robotics & AI` instead

### Cross-sector example
Profile: "Advised hospital systems on operations; also advised a pharmaceutical company on launch strategy"
- `sectors`: `["healthcare", "pharmaceuticals"]` ← two distinct industries
- `matched_sectors`: `["Healthcare, Medical & Social Care", "Pharmaceutical"]`

## PRIMARY EXPERTISE
The category that best represents the person's deepest experience and current professional identity. Must be an EXACT string from `<expertise>`. **Sector names (e.g. "Pharmaceuticals & Life Sciences", "Healthcare", "Financial Services", "Technology & Software") are INVALID here — they belong in `sectors`, not `primary_expertise`.**

**Tiebreak — stop at the FIRST rule that applies:**

0. **Title/practice names MULTIPLE categories** (e.g., "Partner, M&A and Technology Practice"): pick the category with most supporting evidence elsewhere; if still tied, use order in the title (first-named wins).
1. **Title names a single category or practice domain explicitly** → use that category. **This includes practice-head and sector-lead roles.** In consulting firms, a Sector Lead or Practice Head title defines the person's **domain of expertise** — it is NOT merely a description of the client base. A "Global TMT Sector Lead" IS the Technology expert at that firm; their identity and career are built around that domain regardless of what functional work (growth strategy, M&A, etc.) they perform within it. MUST map the named domain to the closest `<expertise>` string and STOP — do NOT read the bio or apply frequency signals afterward.
   - Mapping examples: "TMT" or "Technology" → `Technology`; "M&A" or "Corporate Finance" → `M&A and Corporate Development`; "Operations" or "Supply Chain" → `Operational Improvements`; "Revenue" or "Commercial" or "Growth" → `Revenue Growth`; "Finance" or "CFO" → `Finance and Accounting`.
   - ❌ WRONG: title = "Managing Director, Global Sector Lead, TMT" → bio says "growth strategies" → primary = `Revenue Growth`
   - ✅ CORRECT: title = "Managing Director, Global Sector Lead, TMT" → TMT maps to `Technology` → primary = `Technology` (stop here, bio is irrelevant)
   - If the practice domain has no clear `<expertise>` match, skip to Rule 2.
2. **Current practice group or team name identifies a single category** → use that category.
3. **No explicit signal** → use the category with most evidence across the full profile, weighted toward more recent roles (last 5 years > older experience).

**Justification:** exactly 1 sentence citing the rule applied and the evidence (e.g., "Job title 'Partner, Technology & Digital Infrastructure Practice' directly names the Technology category — Rule 1.").
